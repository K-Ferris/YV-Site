Issues facing pregnant women in South Africa.

Access to maternal care.

More than two thirds of the world's maternal deaths occur in Sub-Saharan Africa, the leading cause of this is inadequate access to quality health care during pregnancy, delivery or after birth. In countries where the rate of maternal mortality is higher, less than half of the women can afford to make it to at least one antenatal visit.

Early Childbearing.

Africa has the highest rates of premature and adolescent childbearing in the world, pregnancy and maternal conditions are the leading causes of death among girls aged 15-19 with the majority occuring in Africa.

Transport.

Progress to reducing maternal mortality in South Africa is hindered by distance and access to health care services, including lack of or poor transport from home to health care. 90% of South Africans live within a 7km radius of the nearest public health facility, however women giving birth before reaching a healthcare facility remains a problem. The lack of reliable and efficient transport from home to a health care facility when experiencing pregnancy complications or when in labour were singled out as the reasons for the high maternal and child deaths and disability.

Our Services


Ambulance

Ideal for patients in extreme situation such as car accidents, pregnancy complications, chronically ill patients and severe violence for example stab wounds



Ambulette

Ideal for those who are in need of assistance but are not in serious condition for example a person who has fallen and broken their arm and is still able to sit upright in a vehicle and also for scheduled appointments .



Helicopter

Ideal for those looking for facility to facility transfer for example transferring between provinces or from international countries to South Africa.



Patient Transfer Transport

Suitable for patients who are having ongoing medical care and are travelling alone or with a care provider, also ideal for hospital visitations from facility to facility.
