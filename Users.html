Why we care.


It is estimated that 60-80 million people are living with disabilities in Africa , and approximately 3 million reside in South Africa (Approximately 7.5% of the population) .

People with disabilities in South Africa in rural areas have more issues and face more barriers when attempting to access healthcare when compared to persons without disabilities.

We support the following disabilities?


Mobility Impairments.

These are persons who's mobility or physical appearance have been altered or disrupted due to an injury or a disease..

Examples: Cerebral Palsy, Muscular Dystrophy, Motor Neuron Disease.



Visual Impairments.

These are persons who's vision or sight have been damaged or altered due to an injury or a disease.

Examples: Blindness, Colour Blindness.



Deaf & Hearing Impairments.

These are persons who have been in an accident resulting in hearing loss or those were either born with or developed hearing problems.

Examples: Tinnitus, Otosclerosis, Vestibular Schwannoma .



AD/HD.

AD/HD affects 1 in 20 children, and an estimated 1 million South African adults, it is misdiagnosed and riddled with confusion regarding how it is treated. It is a long term condition characterized by impulsivity, hyperactivity and difficulties paying attention.

Our Services


Ambulance

Ideal for patients in extreme situation such as car accidents, pregnancy complications, chronically ill patients and severe violence for example stab wounds



Ambulette

Ideal for those who are in need of assistance but are not in serious condition for example a person who has fallen and broken their arm and is still able to sit upright in a vehicle. .



Helicopter

Ideal for those looking for facility to facility transfer for example transferring between provinces or from international countries to South Africa.



Patient Transfer Transport

Suitable for patients who are having ongoing medical care and are travelling alone or with a care provider, also ideal for hospital visitations from facility to facility.
