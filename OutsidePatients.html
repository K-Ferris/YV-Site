Outpatients In South Africa


You are an outpatient when you are not required to spend the night in hospital, once your operation is completed you are free to leave the doctor's office or the hospital.
Though, you might have to wait for anesthesia to wear off or wait to see if there were any complications with the operation.
You are not required to spend the night being observed as long as there are no major consequences

MAJORITY OF HOSPITAL PATIENTS IN SOUTH AFRICA ARE OUTPATIENTS.

Tuberculosis

South Africa ranked 8th globally for the highest TB incidence at a rate of 537 per 100 000 population per year. South Africa is one of the 16 countries that account for 93% of the global TB burden.



Cancer Stats

In 2020 it was estimated that 110 000 new cases of cancer were diagnosed, with about 56 000 cancer related deaths. In 2020 approximately 262 455 members of the population were living with 5 year pervalent cases.



Cardiovascular Disease

In South Africa 68% of women and 31% of men are overweight or obese, with the death rates of non communicable diseases including cardiovascular disease have now risen higher than the HIV/AIDS death rates. The two most frequent forms of cardiovascular disease in South Afrcia are ischaemic heart disease and stroke



HIV/AIDS

HIV and AIDS is still one of the main challenges facing South Africa, approximately 5.7 million citizens are infected with HIV, making South Africa one of the countries with the highest number of infected adults and children.

Our Services


Ambulance

Ideal for patients in extreme situation such as car accidents, pregnancy complications, chronically ill patients and severe violence for example stab wounds



Ambulette

Ideal for those who are in need of assistance but are not in serious condition for example a person who has fallen and broken their arm and is still able to sit upright in a vehicle and also for scheduled appointments .



Helicopter

Ideal for those looking for facility to facility transfer for example transferring between provinces or from international countries to South Africa.



Patient Transfer Transport

Suitable for patients who are having ongoing medical care and are travelling alone or with a care provider, also ideal for hospital visitations from facility to facility.
